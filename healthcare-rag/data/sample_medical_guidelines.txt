# Patient Recovery Guidelines for Dr. Smith's Clinic

## 1. Post-Surgery Care (Appendectomy)
- **Rest and Activity**: For the first 24 hours, limit activity. You may walk around the house slowly to prevent blood clots, but avoid any heavy lifting (more than 10 lbs) for 2 to 4 weeks.
- **Diet**: Start with clear liquids (broth, water, jello) and gradually progress to a normal diet as tolerated. If nausea occurs, revert to clear liquids.
- **Incision Care**: Keep the incision area clean and dry. You may shower after 48 hours, but do not take a bath or swim until your follow-up appointment. Do not apply any creams or ointments to the incision.
- **Pain Management**: Take prescribed pain medication as directed. Do not take aspirin unless specifically approved by Dr. Smith.

## 2. Managing Type 2 Diabetes
- **Blood Sugar Monitoring**: Check your blood glucose levels twice a daily (fasting morning and 2 hours after dinner) unless otherwise directed. Target fasting range is 80-130 mg/dL.
- **Dietary Guidelines**: Focus on a balanced diet rich in vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains. Avoid sugary drinks and processed foods with high glycemic indices.
- **Exercise**: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity (e.g., brisk walking) per week.
- **Medication Routine**: Take Metformin 500mg exactly twice a day with meals as prescribed. If you miss a dose, do not double the next dose.
- **Foot Care**: Inspect feet daily for cuts, blisters, or sores. Wear comfortable, well-fitting shoes.

## 3. General Clinic Policies
- **Late Arrivals**: Patients arriving more than 15 minutes late for their scheduled appointment may need to be rescheduled to ensure fairness to other patients.
- **Prescription Refills**: Please request refills at least 48 hours before you run out of medication. Refills are typically processed during normal business hours (8 AM - 5 PM, Mon-Fri).
- **Emergency After Hours**: For life-threatening emergencies, always dial 911. For non-urgent medical questions after hours, you may leave a message on the nursing line (ext. 201).
